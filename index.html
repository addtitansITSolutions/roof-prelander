Frequently Asked Questions

Have Questions? We've Got Answers

Find answers to some of the most common questions below.

Many homeowners rarely inspect their roofs unless they notice a leak. However, roof damage often begins long before water enters your home. Regular inspections can identify small issues early and help prevent costly repairs later.

A professional roofing contractor can identify damaged shingles, flashing issues, and other potential problems before they become major repairs. Annual inspections also help homeowners plan ahead for future roof replacement when necessary.

Popular roofing materials include asphalt shingles, metal roofing, slate, cedar shakes, and roof tiles. Each material offers different advantages in appearance, durability, energy efficiency, and cost, making it important to choose the right option for your home and climate.

Roofing costs depend on your home's size, roof design, and the materials you choose. Asphalt shingles are generally the most affordable option, while premium materials such as slate or metal roofing require a larger investment. Requesting multiple quotes is the best way to compare pricing.

A new roof can improve curb appeal, increase energy efficiency, and make your home more attractive to buyers. In many cases, homeowners may recover a significant portion of their roofing investment when selling their property, depending on market conditions and location.
